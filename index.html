In Loo — Privacy Policy

In Loo (“the app”, “we”, “us”) is a mobile app for finding and sharing public restrooms. This policy explains what information the app collects, how it is used, and the choices you have. It applies to the In Loo app on iOS and Android.

What we collect

Account information. When you create an account we collect your email address (email sign-up) or phone number (SMS sign-up), plus the profile you choose to create: username, display name, bio, and avatar photo.

Content you post. Restroom listings you publish — including their precise map coordinates, title, description, floor, accessibility details, and photos — as well as check-ins (“visits”), notes, and reports you submit about other listings.

Location. With your permission, the app uses your device’s precise location while you are using it to center the map, show nearby restrooms, and pin new listings. Your location is not stored while you browse. It is stored when you choose to publish a listing (the listing’s coordinates) or check in at a restroom (a timestamped visit record). The app never collects location in the background.

Social graph. Who you follow, your followers, and your close-friends list.

What we do not collect: contacts, advertising identifiers, health or financial data, browsing history, or background location. The app contains no advertising and, at the time of writing, no third-party analytics or tracking SDKs. We do not use your data to track you across other apps or websites.

How we use your information

  • Provide the app: accounts, the restroom map, listings, check-ins, follows.
  • Show your public profile and content to other users (see below).
  • Moderate reported content and enforce our rules.
  • Respond to support requests.
  • Comply with legal obligations.

We do not sell your personal information, and we do not share it with third parties for their own marketing.

Public content — please read

In Loo is a public map. Listings you publish (including their photos and exact coordinates), your profile (username, display name, bio, avatar), and your posts are visible to other users of the app. Setting your account to private limits who can see your activity, such as your check-in history, but listings you publish on the map remain public. Do not publish a location or photo you are not comfortable sharing publicly — in particular, do not create listings at private homes.

Photos are re-encoded on upload, which removes embedded camera metadata such as GPS EXIF tags.

Who processes your data

We use service providers (“processors”) to run the app:

  • Supabase — database, authentication, and file storage. Data is hosted on Supabase infrastructure in the United States.
  • Google — the Google Maps SDK renders the map and receives the coordinates needed to do so; on-device geocoding services convert between addresses and coordinates.

These providers process data on our behalf and under contract. We may also disclose information if required by law, or to protect the safety of users.

If you use the app from outside the United States, your information is transferred to and stored in the United States.

Retention and deletion

Your data is kept while your account exists. When your account is deleted, your profile, listings, photos, check-ins, follows, and reports are deleted with it.

To delete your account or request a copy of your data, email support@inloo.app from the address associated with your account (or include your username). We aim to complete deletion and export requests within 30 days (GDPR) / 45 days (CCPA). An in-app account-deletion option is being added; until then the email channel above is the deletion path.

Your rights

Depending on where you live (including under the GDPR and the CCPA), you may have the right to access, correct, export, or delete your personal information, and the right not to be discriminated against for exercising those rights. Use the contact below; we will verify the request against your account before acting on it.

Security

Traffic between the app and our servers is encrypted in transit (TLS). Database access is controlled with row-level security so users can only modify their own data. No system is perfectly secure; if a breach affecting your data occurs we will notify you as required by law.

Children

In Loo is not directed at children. You must be at least 13 years old (16 in the European Union, unless a parent or guardian consents) to use the app. If we learn that a younger child has created an account, we will delete it.

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy as the app evolves. Material changes will be announced in the app, and the effective date above will change. Continued use of the app after a change means you accept the updated policy.

Contact

Questions, data requests, or complaints: support@inloo.app
